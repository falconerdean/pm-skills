## 2. Google Business Profile Optimization

### Why GBP Is Critical

- 76% of people who search for local services on their smartphone visit a business within 24 hours
- ~65% of Google searches now end without clicks due to AI Overviews and featured snippets, making GBP the primary visibility channel
- GBP optimization often produces faster visibility than traditional organic rankings (results in 4-6 weeks vs. 3-6 months for organic)
- An optimized GBP profile reportedly generates more inquiries than Psychology Today listings

**Sources:** [Goodman Creatives - GBP Optimization](https://goodmancreatives.com/how-to-optimize-your-google-business-profile-for-local-seo-as-a-therapist/), [Reframe Practice - How Clients Find Therapists](https://reframepractice.com/guides/how-clients-find-therapists)

### Category Selection

| Practice Type | Recommended Primary Category | Secondary Categories |
|--------------|------------------------------|---------------------|
| General therapy | "Mental Health Service" or "Psychotherapist" | "Counselor" |
| Couples/Family | "Marriage or Family Therapist" | "Family Counselor" |
| Child/Adolescent | "Child Psychologist" | "Youth Organization" |
| Substance Abuse | "Drug Addiction Treatment Center" | "Counselor" |
| Psychiatry | "Psychiatrist" | "Mental Health Service" |

Primary category selection is the single most important local SEO signal.

**Source:** [Reframe Practice - Local SEO for Therapists](https://reframepractice.com/guides/local-seo-for-therapists)

### Complete Profile Checklist

- **Business Name:** Exact legal name (no keyword stuffing)
- **Address:** Physical location (telehealth-only practices cannot appear in Map Pack)
- **Phone:** Local number preferred over toll-free
- **Website URL:** Link to homepage or specific landing page
- **Hours:** Accurate and updated for holidays
- **Services:** List each service (Individual Therapy, Couples Therapy, EMDR, etc.)
- **Business Description:** 150-200 words with local keywords; describe problems you solve, therapeutic approaches in plain language, geographic specificity
- **Insurance:** List accepted plans
- **Attributes:** Enable all relevant attributes (LGBTQ+-friendly, wheelchair accessible, telehealth available, etc.)

### Visual Content Strategy

- Professional headshot
- Office exterior/signage (helps Google verify location)
- Waiting room and therapy office photos
- Team member photos
- Aim for 10-15+ quality images
- Rename image files descriptively: "anxiety-therapy-office-portland.jpg"
- Refresh periodically to signal activity
- NEVER photograph appointment books, client files, or clients

### Posting Strategy

- Post 2-3 times weekly (minimum 1x/week)
- Content types: service highlights, educational tips, blog announcements, community involvement
- Posts signal to Google that the profile is active and maintained

### Review Management

- Request reviews ethically from satisfied clients who have completed treatment
- Never offer incentives for reviews
- Respond to every review (positive and negative)
- Keep responses brief and generic -- never confirm client status or share identifying details (HIPAA compliance)
- Track review acquisition rate as a KPI

### Timeline to Results

- Ranking improvements: 4-6 weeks
- New client inquiries: 8-12 weeks after proper optimization

## 3. Schema Markup for Healthcare Providers

### Why Schema Matters for Therapists

Schema markup provides a measurable SEO advantage, particularly for AI search tools that use schema to identify and recommend providers. It removes ambiguity by turning content into structured data that search engines and AI can trust.

**Source:** [eseospace - Healthcare Schema Markup Guide](https://eseospace.com/blog/schema-markups-for-medical-and-healthcare-websites/)

### Recommended Schema Types for Therapy Practices

#### Primary: MedicalBusiness or MedicalClinic

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "MedicalBusiness",
  "name": "Serenity Counseling Center",
  "url": "https://serenitycounseling.com",
  "telephone": "+1-555-123-4567",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "456 Wellness Way, Suite 200",
    "addressLocality": "Austin",
    "addressRegion": "TX",
    "postalCode": "78701",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": "30.2672",
    "longitude": "-97.7431"
  },
  "openingHours": "Mo-Fr 09:00-18:00",
  "medicalSpecialty": "Psychiatric",
  "image": "https://serenitycounseling.com/images/office.jpg",
  "priceRange": "$$",
  "sameAs": [
    "https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/therapists/serenity-counseling",
    "https://www.goodtherapy.org/serenity-counseling"
  ]
}
```

#### For Individual Therapist Bio Pages: Physician or Person

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Physician",
  "name": "Dr. Jane Smith, LCSW",
  "url": "https://serenitycounseling.com/jane-smith",
  "telephone": "+1-555-123-4567",
  "medicalSpecialty": "Psychiatry",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "456 Wellness Way, Suite 200",
    "addressLocality": "Austin",
    "addressRegion": "TX",
    "postalCode": "78701"
  },
  "alumniOf": "University of Texas at Austin",
  "availableLanguage": ["English", "Spanish"],
  "worksFor": {
    "@type": "MedicalBusiness",
    "name": "Serenity Counseling Center"
  }
}
```

#### For FAQ Sections: FAQPage

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "What should I expect in my first therapy session?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Your first session is an intake appointment where we'll discuss your concerns, history, and goals for therapy. It typically lasts 50-60 minutes."
      }
    },
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "Do you accept insurance?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "We accept Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, and Cigna. We also offer sliding scale fees for self-pay clients."
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

#### Additional Recommended Types

- **Service:** Mark up individual services (anxiety therapy, couples counseling, etc.)
- **AggregateRating:** Enable star ratings in search results (only mark up reviews actually hosted on your site)
- **BreadcrumbList:** Navigation paths for multi-page sites
- **WebSite / WebPage:** Site-level entities

### Implementation Best Practices

- Use **JSON-LD format** everywhere (Google's preferred format)
- One primary entity per page
- Keep all marked-up details visible on the page and consistent with GBP
- Assign stable @id values to connect entities across pages
- Standardize addresses across all platforms
- Validate using Google Rich Results Test and Schema.org Validator

## 4. Local SEO Strategies

### The Three Local Ranking Factors

Google's local algorithm weighs three factors:

1. **Relevance:** How well your profile matches what someone is searching for (category, description, services listed)
2. **Distance/Proximity:** Physical distance from the searcher (non-negotiable -- you cannot control this)
3. **Prominence:** Reviews, backlinks, citations, domain authority, and overall web presence

**Source:** [Mental Health IT Solutions - Local SEO for Therapists](https://mentalhealthitsolutions.com/blog/local-seo-for-therapists/)

### City + Therapist Keyword Formulas

Build dedicated landing pages for each of these patterns:

- `[Specialty] therapist in [City]` -- e.g., "Anxiety therapist in Portland"
- `[City] [service]` -- e.g., "Denver couples counseling"
- `Therapy for [issue] in [neighborhood]` -- e.g., "Therapy for grief in Buckhead"
- `[Demographic] therapist [city]` -- e.g., "LGBTQ therapist Austin"
- `[Modality] therapy in [city]` -- e.g., "EMDR therapy in Seattle"

### Local Landing Page Structure

Each city/service landing page should include:

1. **H1:** Service + City (e.g., "Anxiety Therapy in Portland, OR")
2. **Clinical approach** described in client language
3. **Neighborhood coverage** mentioned naturally
4. **Embedded Google Map**
5. **Local FAQs** (insurance accepted locally, parking, telehealth availability)
6. **Clear CTA** (book a consultation, call now)
7. **Schema markup** (LocalBusiness or MedicalBusiness)

### NAP Consistency

Name, Address, Phone must be **identical** across all listings, down to punctuation ("St" vs "Street"). Create a NAP reference document and copy-paste to avoid variations. Inconsistencies signal unreliability to Google and can harm rankings.

### Citation Sources (Priority Order)

**Tier 1 -- Must Have:**
- Google Business Profile
- Psychology Today
- Bing Places
- Apple Maps
- Yelp

**Tier 2 -- Therapy-Specific:**
- TherapyDen
- GoodTherapy
- Healthgrades
- OpenCounseling
- Theravive

**Tier 3 -- Local Authority:**
- Chamber of Commerce
- State licensing board directory
- Local professional associations
- University/hospital affiliations

### Local Backlink Building (Priority Tiers)

| Difficulty | Sources |
|-----------|---------|
| Easy | Chamber of Commerce, local directories, professional associations |
| Medium | Guest blogs on local wellness sites, podcast appearances, local news features |
| High-authority | University psychology departments, hospital referral pages, nonprofit partnerships |

"A dozen high-authority links from relevant sources outweigh hundreds of low-quality links."

### Telehealth Considerations

- Telehealth-only practices **cannot appear in the Map Pack** without a physical business address
- Can still rank in organic results
- Service area selection should only include regions where you realistically serve clients

### Timeline

- GBP changes propagate: 1-2 weeks
- Directory citation propagation: 2-4 weeks
- Meaningful local ranking improvements: 4-8 weeks once aligned
- Competitive organic rankings: 3-6 months

### Content Writing Best Practices

1. **Write to one person** -- picture your ideal client and write as if explaining something in conversation
2. **Use symptom language, not clinical jargon** -- "thoughts keep looping until your chest gets tight" beats "anxiety disorders treated with CBT"
3. **Answer specific questions** -- structure content with clear questions as headings and direct answers near the top
4. **Cite reputable sources** -- NIMH, APA, PubMed, peer-reviewed journals
5. **Add "Last Reviewed On" dates** to health articles
6. **Include author bylines** with credentials linking to detailed bio pages
7. **Internal link** from blog posts back to service pages using descriptive anchor text

### Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) for AI Search

In 2026, therapists need to optimize for AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) in addition to traditional search:

- State answers plainly and define terms clearly
- Use question-and-answer format
- Back claims with specifics rather than marketing language
- Content optimized for AEO also performs well in traditional SEO
- Structured, factual content is more likely to be recommended by AI tools

**Key stat:** 527% year-over-year increase in AI engine referrals to therapy-related websites.

## 6. Technical SEO Requirements

### Core Web Vitals Thresholds (2026)

| Metric | Good | Needs Improvement | Poor |
|--------|------|--------------------|------|
| **LCP** (Largest Contentful Paint) | < 2.5 seconds | 2.5-4.0 seconds | > 4.0 seconds |
| **INP** (Interaction to Next Paint) | < 200ms | 200-500ms | > 500ms |
| **CLS** (Cumulative Layout Shift) | < 0.1 | 0.1-0.25 | > 0.25 |
| **TTFB** (Time to First Byte) | < 200ms | 200-500ms | > 500ms |

Google evaluates the 75th percentile of all page loads -- meaning 75% of visits must meet the "good" threshold. Only 47% of sites currently reach Google's "good" thresholds.

**Key stat:** A one-second delay in page load time reduces conversions by 7%. 53% of users abandon a site that takes longer than 3 seconds to load.

**Sources:** [ClickRank - Core Web Vitals 2026](https://www.clickrank.ai/core-web-vitals-impact-on-seo-rankings/), [UnfoldMart - Core Web Vitals 2026](https://www.unfoldmart.com/blogs/core-web-vitals-2026)

### Technical SEO Checklist for Therapy Websites

#### Essential (Non-Negotiable)
- [ ] SSL certificate (HTTPS) -- mandatory for YMYL health content
- [ ] Mobile-responsive design (mobile-first indexing is standard)
- [ ] Page load speed under 3 seconds
- [ ] XML sitemap submitted to Google Search Console
- [ ] Google Search Console connected and monitored
- [ ] Google Analytics installed
- [ ] No broken links (404 errors)
- [ ] HIPAA-compliant contact forms with BAA

#### On-Page Requirements
- [ ] Unique meta title and description on every page
- [ ] Single H1 per page (include primary keyword)
- [ ] Logical H2/H3 heading hierarchy
- [ ] Descriptive alt text on all images (include location + service keywords)
- [ ] Image files renamed descriptively (not IMG_1234.jpg)
- [ ] Image compression for fast loading
- [ ] Internal linking between related pages
- [ ] Schema markup on key pages (LocalBusiness, FAQPage, Physician)

#### Site Architecture
- [ ] Clear navigation with service pages accessible in 2-3 clicks
- [ ] Dedicated page for each specialty/service
- [ ] Dedicated page for each location served
- [ ] About/bio page with credentials prominently displayed
- [ ] Contact page with embedded map, phone, and scheduling

#### Security & Compliance
- [ ] HIPAA-compliant scheduling tools (with BAA)
- [ ] HIPAA-compliant email/contact forms (Hushmail, Paubox, etc.)
- [ ] Published Privacy Policy
- [ ] HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices
- [ ] Transparent data handling disclosures

### Platform Considerations

Most common therapy website platforms (Squarespace, WordPress, Wix) handle basic technical SEO automatically. Focus effort on:
- Content-level factors (meta tags, headings, content quality)
- Image optimization
- Schema markup implementation
- Page speed optimization

## 8. E-E-A-T & YMYL Guidelines for Therapy Websites

### Why Therapy Sites Face Higher Scrutiny

Google classifies mental health and therapy content as **YMYL (Your Money or Your Life)** because it "can significantly impact a person's health, happiness, safety, or financial stability." This classification triggers the highest level of scrutiny from Google's quality evaluation systems.

Incorrect mental health information can cause real harm. Google's algorithms and human quality raters hold YMYL content to a higher standard than general content. Generic, thin, or unverifiable health content will be suppressed in favor of content from demonstrable experts.

**Sources:** [eseospace - YMYL and E-E-A-T](https://eseospace.com/blog/ymyl-and-e-e-a-t/), [Crownsvillemedia - YMYL and E-E-A-T for Therapists](https://crownsvillemedia.com/therapist-ymyl-and-e-e-a-t)

### The E-E-A-T Framework for Therapists

#### Experience (First-Hand Knowledge)

**What Google looks for:** Evidence that the content creator has real, first-hand experience with the topic.

**How therapists demonstrate it:**
- Share anonymized case studies showing real therapeutic situations
- Write from clinical experience: "In my practice, I've found that clients with high-functioning anxiety often..."
- Discuss specific challenges encountered in practice
- Include personal insights on how you apply evidence-based techniques
- Blog from a first-person clinical perspective, not textbook summaries

#### Expertise (Qualified Knowledge)

**What Google looks for:** Verifiable credentials proving the author is a qualified expert.

**How therapists demonstrate it:**
- Create detailed "About Me" pages with: full name, license type (LPC, LCSW, LMFT, PhD, PsyD), license number, education, certifications, years of experience, specializations
- Include professional headshot
- Attach author bylines with credentials to all blog content
- Link bylines to detailed bio pages
- List continuing education and specialized training
- Display credentials in page footer or header

#### Authoritativeness (Recognized Standing)

**What Google looks for:** Recognition from other authoritative sources as a leader or go-to source.

**How therapists demonstrate it:**
- Backlinks from reputable sources (directories, therapy publications, local news, universities)
- Directory listings on Psychology Today, GoodTherapy, TherapyDen
- Consistent cross-citations across platforms
- Publications, speaking engagements, media appearances
- Guest posts on other health/wellness websites
- Professional organization memberships
- Podcast appearances

#### Trustworthiness (Most Important Factor)

**What Google looks for:** Accuracy, transparency, and security. Google has stated that Trustworthiness is the most important E-E-A-T component.

**How therapists demonstrate it:**
- Install SSL certificates (HTTPS mandatory)
- Use HIPAA-compliant contact forms and scheduling tools with BAA
- Display physical address, phone, and email prominently
- Publish accessible Privacy Policy and HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices
- Cite reputable sources (NIMH, APA, WHO, PubMed, peer-reviewed journals)
- Avoid exaggerated claims ("guaranteed results," "cure your anxiety")
- Add "Last Reviewed On" dates to health articles
- Maintain identical business information across all platforms
- Clearly explain data handling procedures

### Content Standards Checklist for YMYL Compliance

- [ ] Every health-related article has a named, credentialed author
- [ ] Author bio page exists with verifiable license information
- [ ] Claims are backed by citations to reputable sources
- [ ] Content is written compassionately in accessible language
- [ ] No exaggerated claims or guaranteed outcomes
- [ ] "Last Reviewed" or "Last Updated" dates displayed
- [ ] Site has HTTPS (SSL certificate)
- [ ] Contact information is prominently displayed
- [ ] Privacy Policy and HIPAA notices are published
- [ ] Content reflects genuine clinical experience, not generic summaries

### AI-Generated Content Warning

Google does not ban AI-generated content, but in YMYL categories, AI-written articles must be:
- Carefully reviewed and edited by a licensed clinician
- Fact-checked against current clinical evidence
- Written with a unique clinical voice and perspective
- Attributed to a real, credentialed author

"Cookie-cutter" or generic AI content is increasingly penalized, especially in health categories.
